<p>The <strong>5,205 GWh</strong> of <strong>renewable heat</strong> <strong>output</strong> in Scotland in <strong>2019</strong> comes from <strong>2.03 GW</strong> of <strong>capacity</strong> across <strong>30,450</strong> <strong>installations</strong>.&nbsp;</p>
<p>As in 2018, the majority of both&nbsp;<strong>capacity</strong>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<strong>output</strong>&nbsp;of renewable heat in&nbsp;<strong>2019</strong>&nbsp;came from&nbsp;<strong>biomass</strong>&nbsp;primary combustion and biomass combined heat and power (CHP). Together, these technologies account for&nbsp;<strong>1.65 GW</strong>&nbsp;of&nbsp;<strong>capacity</strong>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<strong>3,678 GWh</strong>&nbsp;of&nbsp;<strong>output (70%)</strong>.&nbsp;<strong>Biomethane</strong> makes up <strong>14%</strong> of <strong>output</strong>, followed by <strong>heat pumps</strong> (<strong>8%</strong>) and <strong>energy from waste</strong>&nbsp;(<strong>7%</strong><strong>)</strong>.</p>
<p>Almost half (<strong>44%,</strong>&nbsp;<strong>2,313 GWh</strong>) of renewable heat output comes from 90 large&nbsp;<strong>installations</strong>&nbsp;of&nbsp;<strong>over 1 MW</strong>. There are&nbsp;<strong>less than ten</strong>&nbsp;installations that&nbsp;<strong>generated more than 100 GWh</strong>&nbsp;in 2019; together these sites provided 25% (<strong>1,297 GWh</strong>) of the total renewable heat output in Scotland in&nbsp;<strong>2019</strong>.</p>
<p><strong>26,510</strong> (<strong>87%</strong>) of the renewable heat installations are <strong>micro</strong> installations of less than 45 kW.&nbsp; It is believed that most of these will be in the domestic sector.</p>
