<p>Oil and gas remains an integral part of Scotland&rsquo;s energy system. The chart looks at the <strong>primary energy, </strong>in other words, fuels as they enter the Scottish energy system. This refers to the all the energy produced within Scotland plus all the energy imported into Scotland.</p>
<p><strong>Oil </strong>accounts for more than half of primary energy (<strong>62.2%</strong>), with <strong>gas </strong>making up <strong>29.5% </strong>and <strong>petroleum products </strong>making up <strong>2.5% </strong>in <strong>2019</strong>. This makes up a total of <strong>93.5% </strong>of Scotland&rsquo;s primary energy (encompassing all of Scotland&rsquo;s indigenous production and imports). This has fallen from 94.3% the previous year.</p>
