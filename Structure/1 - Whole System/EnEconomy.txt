<p>Scotland&rsquo;s <strong>energy</strong> (including renewables) <strong>sector</strong> was identified in <a href="https://www.gov.scot/publications/scotlands-economic-strategy/">Scotland&rsquo;s Economic Strategy </a>as one of the growth sectors in which Scotland can build on existing comparative advantage and increase productivity and growth. The sector is defined using the <strong>Standard Industrialisation Classification (SIC) 2007</strong> codes that can be found <a href="https://www2.gov.scot/Resource/0054/00547085.docx">here.</a> Due to the sector definitions, the figures are not directly comparable with those on the low carbon and renewable sector or the oil and gas sector.</p>
<p><strong><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Gross value added (GVA)</span></strong> for the Energy growth sector totalled <strong>&pound;13.6 billion</strong> in <strong>2020, down 31.9%</strong>&nbsp;on <strong>2019</strong>.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Total&nbsp;<span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>turnover </strong></span>in&nbsp;the Scottish energy sector was&nbsp;<strong>&pound;33.3 billion </strong>in&nbsp;<strong>2020, down 16.8%</strong>&nbsp;in nominal terms on&nbsp;<strong>2019</strong>.</p>
<p>Total <span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>exports</strong> </span>from the Energy growth sector stood at <strong>&pound;15.6 billion</strong> in <strong>2019</strong>, accounting for <strong>18%</strong> of <strong>Scotland&rsquo;s total exports</strong>. Exports from this sector <strong>increased</strong> by <strong>11%</strong> over the latest year.</p>
<p><span style="text-decoration: underline;"><strong>Employment</strong> </span>in the energy sector stood at <strong>69,000 in 2020</strong>, unchanged from&nbsp;<strong>2019.</strong> <strong>Scotland</strong> accounted for <strong>21%</strong> of employment in Energy across <strong>Great Britain</strong> as a whole. Employment in the energy sector is highly <strong>concentrated</strong> in the <strong>North-East </strong>with<strong> 35%</strong> of all jobs in <strong>Aberdeen City</strong> and <strong>14%</strong> in <strong>Aberdeenshire.</strong></p>
