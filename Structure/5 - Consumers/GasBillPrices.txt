<p>Like with electricity, average domestic gas prices <strong>increased </strong>in real terms compared to their <strong>lowest point </strong>in <strong>2001; direct debit </strong>and <strong>standard credit </strong>customers&rsquo; bills almost <strong>doubled</strong>, and <strong>prepayment</strong> customers increased by <strong>78.0%</strong>. The cost of gas rose at a faster rate than electricity. Scottish gas prices <strong>peaked</strong> in <strong>2014</strong>, and by <strong>2019</strong> <strong>dropped</strong> <strong>by</strong> approximately <strong>20%</strong> on this peak.</p>
<p><strong>Direct debit</strong> customers have the <strong>lowest</strong> bills in <strong>2019</strong> and <strong>standard credit</strong> the <strong>highest</strong>, <strong>10.4% more</strong>. Even though <strong>standard credit</strong> customers have the highest bills, they <strong>dropped</strong> by <strong>2.9%</strong> from <strong>2018</strong> and <strong>2019</strong>. In the same period, <strong>prepayment </strong>customers <strong>increased</strong> by <strong>7.1%</strong>.</p>
<p>Differences between bills in North and South Scotland are negligible, and both regions are marginally lower than the British total.</p>
