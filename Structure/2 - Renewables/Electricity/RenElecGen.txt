<p>Looking at the latest quarter, <strong>11,147 GWh</strong> of renewable electricity was generated in <strong>2022 Q1</strong>, up <strong>24%</strong> from <strong>2021 Q1</strong>. This most likely came from an increase in the amount of wind in Q1 2022 compared to Q1 2021.&nbsp;</p>
<p>In <strong>2021</strong>, <strong>27.5 TWh of renewable electricity</strong> was <strong>generated</strong> in Scotland. This is a <strong>14.3% decrease</strong> compared to <strong>2020</strong>, which is likely due to the milder weather throughout the year.</p>
<p><strong>Scotland</strong> made up almost a quarter (<strong>22.3%</strong>) of the <strong>UK</strong>&rsquo;s renewable electricity generation in 2021.</p>
<p>The majority of Scotland&rsquo;s renewable electricity generation continues to come from <strong>wind</strong> (<strong>20.0 TWh </strong>in <strong>2021</strong>). <strong>2.7 TWh</strong> was generated via offshore wind, down from <strong>3.5 TWh</strong> in <strong>2020</strong>. <strong>17.3 TWh</strong> was generated by onshore wind.</p>
