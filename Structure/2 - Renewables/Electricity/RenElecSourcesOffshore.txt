<p><strong>Offshore wind</strong> capacity is <strong>2.1 GW </strong>as of September 2022.&nbsp; <strong>2.7&nbsp;TWh</strong> of electricity was generated via offshore wind in <strong>2021, </strong>down from <strong>3.5&nbsp;TWh </strong>in <strong>2020</strong>.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Offshore wind is likely to play more of a role in the future; <strong>10 </strong>offshore wind projects totalling <strong>3.6 GW</strong> are in&nbsp;<strong>development</strong>.&nbsp; <strong>1.9 GW</strong> is&nbsp; currently <strong>under construction</strong>.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Electricity generation via offshore wind may have generated&nbsp;<strong>&pound;0.62 billion</strong>&nbsp;in turnover in&nbsp;<strong>2020</strong>, but has directly supported approximately&nbsp;<strong>2,200 </strong>full-time equivalent&nbsp;<strong>jobs&nbsp;</strong>in Scotland.</p>
