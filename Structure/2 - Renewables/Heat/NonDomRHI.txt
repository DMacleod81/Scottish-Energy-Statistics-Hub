<p>The Renewable Heat Incentive (RHI) is a UK Government scheme set up to encourage uptake of renewable heat technologies amongst householders, communities and businesses through financial incentives. The non-domestic scheme launched in November 2011.</p>
<p>As of <strong>February 2020</strong>, <strong>3,816</strong> accredited full applications were made, with <strong>118</strong> applications made in the preceding twelve months. Capacity of accredited applications more than quadrupled from <strong>250 MW</strong> in <strong>February 2015</strong> to <strong>1.02 GW</strong> in <strong>February 2020</strong>.</p>
<p>More than <strong>7,100 GWh</strong> of heat was generated under the non-domestic scheme as of <strong>June 2019</strong>. <strong>Biomass</strong> boilers account for the majority of this (<strong>72.3%</strong>), with <strong>21.7%</strong> of all generation from <strong>biomethane</strong> injected into the <strong>gas grid</strong>.</p>
