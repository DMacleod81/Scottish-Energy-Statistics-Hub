<p>Even with the growth of renewables in recent years, oil and gas continue to make up most of Scotland&rsquo;s energy consumption. In 2020:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Oil and petroleum </strong>make up <strong>42.0%</strong> of all Scottish consumption and <strong>gas </strong>makes up a third (<strong>35.6%</strong>). Just <strong>22.4% </strong>of all Scottish consumption comes from <strong>other fuels</strong>. This means that <strong>oil and gas </strong>make up <strong>77.6% </strong>of <strong>all Scottish consumption, </strong>slightly higher than <strong>73.3% </strong>for the <strong>UK </strong>as a whole<strong>.</strong></li>
<li>In the <strong>domestic </strong>sector in Scotland, <strong>gas </strong>makes up two-thirds of all consumption (<strong>67.4%</strong>).</li>
<li>Oil and gas makes up <strong>92.2% </strong>of Scotland's <strong>heat </strong>demand.</li>
</ul>
