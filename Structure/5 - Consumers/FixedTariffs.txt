<p>On a fixed tariff, customers&rsquo; unit rates and standing charges do not change for the duration of the contract.&nbsp; This means that households are protected from energy price rises, and their bills only differ depending on the energy consumed.&nbsp;</p>
<p>The proportion of customers on fixed tariffs across <strong>Great Britain</strong> decreased over the course of 2022.&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li>In <strong>North Scotland</strong>, the proportion of <strong>Gas</strong> customers on a fixed tariff decreased from <strong>32%</strong> in December 2021 to <strong>16%</strong> in March 2023. The proportion of <strong>Electricity</strong> customers on a fixed tariff increased from <strong>36%</strong> in December 2021 to <strong>45%</strong> in March 2023.</li>
</ul>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<ul>
<li>In <strong>South Scotland</strong>, The proportion of <strong>Gas</strong> customers on a fixed tariff decreased from <strong>33%</strong> in December 2021 to <strong>29%</strong> in March 2023. The proportion of <strong>Electricity</strong> customers on a fixed tariff decreased from<strong> 29%</strong> December 2021 to <strong>24%</strong> in March 2023.</li>
</ul>
