<p>In <strong>2021</strong>, <strong>27.2 TWh of renewable electricity</strong> was <strong>generated</strong> in Scotland. This is a <strong>15.1% decrease</strong> compared to <strong>2020</strong>, which is likely due to the milder weather throughout the year.</p>
<p>This is the equivalent of <strong>powering all households</strong> in Scotland for almost <strong>three years</strong>, or charging almost 6 billion mobile phones for a year.</p>
<p><strong>Scotland</strong> made up almost a quarter (<strong>22.3%</strong>) of the <strong>UK</strong>&rsquo;s renewable electricity generation in 2021.</p>
<p>The majority of Scotland&rsquo;s renewable electricity generation continues to come from <strong>wind</strong> (<strong>19.9&nbsp;TWh </strong>in <strong>2021</strong>). <strong>2.7 TWh</strong> was generated via offshore wind, down from <strong>3.2 TWh</strong> in <strong>2020</strong>. <strong>17.2 TWh</strong> was generated by onshore wind.</p>
