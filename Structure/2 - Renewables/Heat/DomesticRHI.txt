<p>The Renewable Heat Incentive (RHI) is a UK Government scheme set up to encourage uptake of renewable heat technologies amongst householders, communities and businesses through financial incentives. The domestic scheme launched in April 2014.</p>
<p>By <strong>May 2020</strong>, there were <strong>15,442</strong>&nbsp;accredited domestic RHI applications in Scotland, <strong>up 13.8%</strong> on <strong>May 2019</strong>. <strong>2019</strong> had the highest number of applications in a single year since 2016 with <strong>1,759</strong> applications, <strong>up 70.6%</strong> on <strong>2018</strong>.</p>
<p>More than half (<strong>58.3%</strong>) of accredited applications are for <strong>air source heat pumps</strong>, and of all technologies, this has had the biggest increase in applications in the last twelve months. <strong>Biomass</strong> makes up just a quarter (<strong>24.4%</strong>) of all accredited applications.</p>
<p>Scotland has <strong>1.1 TWh</strong> of heat paid for under the domestic scheme as of <strong>May 2020</strong>.</p>
