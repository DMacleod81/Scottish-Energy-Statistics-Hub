<p>From a&nbsp;<strong>baseline</strong>&nbsp;of&nbsp;<strong>22.7 million tonnes of CO2</strong>, greenhouse gas<strong>&nbsp;emissions</strong>&nbsp;from the&nbsp;<strong>energy supply</strong>&nbsp;sector have&nbsp;<strong>fallen 70.1%</strong>&nbsp;to&nbsp;<strong>6.8 million tonnes of CO2</strong>&nbsp;in&nbsp;<strong>2018</strong>. This in large part due to changes in&nbsp;<strong>electricity production</strong>, where a&nbsp;<strong>reduction</strong>&nbsp;in the use of&nbsp;<strong>fossil fuels</strong>&nbsp;for electricity generation has led to an&nbsp;<strong>85.4%</strong>&nbsp;<strong>decrease</strong>&nbsp;in emissions to&nbsp;<strong>2018</strong>.</p>
<p>Greenhouse gas emissions associated with industry have broadly&nbsp;<strong>remained stable </strong><strong>for the past </strong><strong>20 years, </strong><strong>falling from </strong><strong>6.6</strong> <strong>million tonnes of CO2</strong><strong> in </strong><strong>1999</strong><strong> to a low of </strong><strong>4.1 million tonnes of CO2 </strong><strong>in</strong><strong> 2014, </strong><strong>before reaching</strong><strong> 4.7 million tonnes of CO2 </strong><strong>in</strong><strong> 2018.</strong></p>
