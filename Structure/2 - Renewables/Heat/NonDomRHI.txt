<p>The Renewable Heat Incentive (RHI) is a UK Government scheme set up to encourage uptake of renewable heat technologies amongst householders, communities and businesses through financial incentives. The non-domestic scheme launched in November 2011.</p>
<p>As of&nbsp;<strong>September 2022, 4,197 </strong>accredited full applications were made, with <strong>114 </strong>applications made in the preceding twelve months. This is <strong>18.9%</strong> of all accredited full applications in GB. the majority of the accredited full applications are from buildings <strong>off the gas grid</strong> (<strong>77.3%</strong> as of December 2021).&nbsp; Capacity of accredited applications more than quadrupled from <strong>250 MW</strong> in <strong>February 2015</strong> to <strong>1,128 MW</strong> by <strong>May 2022</strong>.</p>
<p><strong>16.1 TWh</strong> of heat was generated under the non-domestic scheme as of <strong>September 2022</strong>. <strong>Biomass</strong> boilers account for the majority of this (<strong>65.8%</strong>), with <strong>24.2%</strong> of all generation from <strong>biomethane</strong> injected into the <strong>gas grid</strong>.</p>
