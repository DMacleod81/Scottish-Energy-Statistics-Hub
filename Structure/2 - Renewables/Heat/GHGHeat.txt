<p>Greenhouse gas emissions associated with heat specifically relating to buildings has <strong>fallen</strong>, going from <strong>11.2 million tonnes</strong> of <strong>CO2</strong> in <strong>1998</strong> to <strong>8.2 million tonnes</strong> of <strong>CO2</strong> in <strong>2021</strong>, a <strong>reduction</strong> of <strong>26.9%.</strong></p>
<p>The <strong>majority</strong> of these emissions (<strong>75.1%</strong>) are from <strong>residential </strong>buildings, followed by emissions from <strong>business and industrial</strong> processes (<strong>13.8%</strong>) and <strong>public sector</strong> buildings (<strong>11.2%</strong>).</p>
<p>Overall, the <strong>proportion</strong> of greenhouse gas emissions which are related to heat has <strong>slightly risen</strong>, going from <strong>13.8%</strong> in <strong>1998</strong> to <strong>19.7</strong><strong>%</strong> in <strong>2021</strong>.</p>
