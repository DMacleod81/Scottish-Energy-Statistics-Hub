<p>The Renewable Heat Incentive (RHI) is a UK Government scheme set up to encourage uptake of renewable heat technologies amongst householders, communities and businesses through financial incentives. The domestic scheme launched in April 2014.</p>
<p>By <strong>March 2020</strong>, there were <strong>15,224</strong>&nbsp;accredited domestic RHI applications in Scotland, <strong>up 14.0%</strong> on <strong>March 2019</strong>. <strong>2019</strong> had the highest number of applications in a single year since 2016 with <strong>1,759</strong> applications, <strong>up 70.6%</strong> on <strong>2018</strong>.</p>
<p>More than half (<strong>57.6%</strong>) of accredited applications are for <strong>air source heat pumps</strong>, and of all technologies, this has had the biggest increase in applications in the last twelve months. <strong>Biomass</strong> makes up just a quarter (<strong>24.9%</strong>) of all accredited applications.</p>
<p>Scotland has more than <strong>870 GWh</strong> of heat paid for under the domestic scheme as of <strong>June 2019</strong>.</p>
