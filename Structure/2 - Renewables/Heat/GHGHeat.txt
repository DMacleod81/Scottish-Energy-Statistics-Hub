<p>Greenhouse gas emissions associated with heat specifically relating to buildings has <strong>fallen</strong>, going from <strong>11.1 million tonnes</strong> of <strong>CO2</strong> in <strong>1998</strong> to <strong>8.5 million tonnes</strong> of <strong>CO2</strong> in <strong>2018</strong>, a <strong>reduction</strong> of <strong>23.6%.</strong></p>
<p>The <strong>majority</strong> of these emissions (<strong>71.7%</strong>) are from <strong>residential </strong>buildings, followed by emissions from <strong>business and industrial</strong> processes (<strong>15.4%</strong>) and <strong>public sector</strong> buildings (<strong>13.0%</strong>).</p>
<p>Overall, the <strong>proportion</strong> of greenhouse gas emissions which are related to heat has <strong>slightly risen</strong>, going from <strong>14.6%</strong> in <strong>1998</strong> to <strong>20.3%</strong> in <strong>2018</strong>.</p>
