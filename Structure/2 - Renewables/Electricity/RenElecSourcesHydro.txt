<p>Scotland has a long established base of&nbsp;<strong>hydro</strong>&nbsp;capacity; in 2000, installed capacity was 1,320 MW.&nbsp; By <strong>December 2019</strong>, this grew by just 25% to&nbsp;<strong>1,654 MW</strong>.&nbsp; Just&nbsp;<strong>32 MW&nbsp;</strong>of hydro&nbsp;<strong>capacity&nbsp;</strong>is in the&nbsp;<strong>pipeline</strong>.&nbsp;</p>
<p>In 2019, Scotland generated almost <strong>5,400 GWh&nbsp;</strong>via hydro, the equivalent of powering more than <strong>half&nbsp;</strong>of&nbsp;<strong>Scotland&rsquo;s homes&nbsp;</strong>for a year.&nbsp; This is <strong>up</strong> by <strong>7.7%</strong> on <strong>2018</strong>, possibly as a result of greater rainfall in 2019.</p>
<p><strong>Scotland&nbsp;</strong>accounts for&nbsp;<strong>90.5%&nbsp;</strong>of all hydro generation in the&nbsp;<strong>UK</strong>. &nbsp;</p>
