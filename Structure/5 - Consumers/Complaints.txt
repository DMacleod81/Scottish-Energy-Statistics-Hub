<p>Ombudsman Services published their <a href="https://assets.ctfassets.net/46t2drav2f3e/464DvZ3MR1EcND0y3WTylm/72746e78135fb4ae508cbf56565c6da6/1192_Energy_Report_v8.pdf">Energy Sector Report</a> for <strong>2019</strong> in August 2020.&nbsp; <strong>Complaints</strong> data requested specifically for Scotland from the Ombudsman shows that:</p>
<ul>
<li>Scotland accounted for <strong>11%</strong> of Ombudsman Services Energy complaints <strong>resolved</strong> in <strong>2019</strong> which equates to <strong>6,211</strong> cases.</li>
<li><strong>Glasgow, Edinburgh</strong> and <strong>Motherwell</strong> made up half <strong>(51%</strong>) of Scotland complaints when viewing by district.</li>
<li><strong>Billing</strong> complaints accounted for over half of all Scottish energy&nbsp;complaints (<strong>58%</strong>), followed by <strong>metering </strong>(<strong>9%</strong>) and <strong>transfering&nbsp;</strong>(also <strong>9%</strong>).</li>
<li><strong>60%</strong> of cases resulted in an outcome where the Ombudsman found <strong>in favour of the complainant</strong> (<strong>upheld</strong>).&nbsp; <strong>24%</strong> of complaints were <strong>settled</strong>.</li>
</ul>
