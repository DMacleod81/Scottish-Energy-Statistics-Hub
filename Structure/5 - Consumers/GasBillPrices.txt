<p>Gas bill prices have <strong>increased</strong>, in cash terms, for standard credit (up <strong>4.5%</strong> from 2020) and direct debit (up <strong>5.2%</strong> from 2020) customers since 2020. In the same period, gas bill prices have <strong>decreased</strong>, in cash terms, for prepayment customers (down <strong>4.3%</strong> from 2020).</p>
<p><strong>Prepayment </strong>customers, in 2021, paid on average the <strong>lowest</strong>&nbsp;<strong>gas bills</strong> in Scotland, <strong>11.3% lower</strong>&nbsp;than&nbsp;<strong>standard credit</strong> customers who paid the most, and <strong>2.2% lower</strong>&nbsp;than <strong>direct debit </strong>customers.</p>
<p>Differences between bills in North and South Scotland are negligible, and both regions are marginally higher than the British average.</p>
